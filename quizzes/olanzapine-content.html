Olanzapine (Zyprexa): High Efficacy, High Metabolic Cost

10 questions
1. Olanzapine is contraindicated within 1 hour of IM lorazepam due to risk of:
A. Hypertensive crisis
B. Serotonin syndrome
C. Fatal respiratory depression and cardiovascular collapse
D. Neuroleptic malignant syndrome
Combining IM olanzapine with IM benzodiazepines (especially within 1 hour) has caused fatal respiratory depression and cardiovascular collapse. This combination is explicitly contraindicated. If a benzodiazepine was recently given IM, allow at least 1 hour before IM olanzapine or use IM haloperidol instead.
2. Which receptor blockade is MOST responsible for olanzapine's high rate of weight gain?
A. D2
B. 5-HT2C and H1
C. α1
D. M1
5-HT2C antagonism increases appetite by disinhibiting hypothalamic feeding circuits. H1 histamine blockade also promotes weight gain via central hunger pathways. Together, these are the primary drivers of olanzapine's significant metabolic burden (average +4.2 kg in short-term trials).
3. In the CATIE trial, olanzapine demonstrated the lowest all-cause discontinuation rate among SGAs. What was the main reason it was not universally chosen?
A. Inferior efficacy for positive symptoms
B. Higher rates of metabolic adverse effects and weight gain
C. Significantly higher EPS than other SGAs
D. High cost compared to other agents
While CATIE showed olanzapine had the lowest overall discontinuation rate (suggesting superior effectiveness), it had significantly higher metabolic discontinuation rates due to weight gain, glucose elevation, and dyslipidemia. This metabolic profile remains the primary limitation in its clinical use.
4. Olanzapine-samidorphan (Lybalvi) was designed to reduce weight gain. Samidorphan's mechanism is:
A. D2 partial agonism reduces metabolic signaling
B. Mu-opioid receptor antagonism reducing appetite
C. Metformin-like insulin sensitization
D. GLP-1 receptor agonism
Samidorphan is a mu-opioid receptor antagonist. Opioid receptor antagonism in reward and appetite circuits counteracts the weight gain driven by olanzapine's 5-HT2C and H1 blockade, without reducing antipsychotic efficacy.
5. Post-injection delirium/sedation syndrome (PDSS) is associated with which olanzapine formulation?
A. Olanzapine ODT (Zyprexa Zydis)
B. Olanzapine IM
C. Olanzapine pamoate LAI (Zyprexa Relprevv)
D. Olanzapine + fluoxetine (Symbyax)
Olanzapine pamoate LAI carries risk of post-injection delirium/sedation syndrome (PDSS), a rare event where olanzapine accidentally enters the bloodstream during injection causing acute oversedation, delirium, or coma. ALL patients must be observed for ≥3 hours post-injection at a registered healthcare facility.
6. Symbyax is a combination of which two agents, FDA-approved for bipolar depression?
A. Olanzapine + sertraline
B. Olanzapine + fluoxetine
C. Olanzapine + lithium
D. Olanzapine + lamotrigine
Symbyax is the fixed-dose combination of olanzapine and fluoxetine (OFC). It is FDA-approved for bipolar I depression and treatment-resistant depression. The antidepressant mechanism of fluoxetine combined with olanzapine's mood-stabilizing properties addresses the complex pharmacology of bipolar depression.
7. Per APA metabolic monitoring guidelines, fasting glucose should be checked at baseline and then repeated at which interval on olanzapine?
A. Every 2 weeks
B. 4 weeks only
C. 12 weeks, then annually
D. Only if symptoms develop
APA monitoring protocol: fasting glucose at baseline, then at 12 weeks, then annually for patients on antipsychotics. Weight checks are more frequent (baseline, 4 wks, 8 wks, 12 wks, quarterly). This front-loading addresses the rapid early metabolic changes with high-risk antipsychotics like olanzapine.
8. Olanzapine's ODT (orally disintegrating tablet) formulation is most useful for:
A. Faster onset of action vs. standard tablet
B. Patients who 'cheeking' — hiding tablets to avoid taking them
C. Reduced metabolic risk
D. Lower doses of olanzapine
Olanzapine ODT (Zyprexa Zydis) dissolves on the tongue and cannot be cheeked (hidden in the mouth) like a standard tablet. It is pharmacokinetically equivalent to the regular tablet but is useful for adherence monitoring and in patients who are suspected of not swallowing oral medications.
9. Which of the following is the MOST metabolically favorable alternative when switching a patient off olanzapine?
A. Quetiapine
B. Clozapine
C. Aripiprazole
D. Iloperidone
Aripiprazole is among the most metabolically neutral antipsychotics — minimal weight gain, minimal glucose and lipid effects. When switching from olanzapine due to metabolic concerns, aripiprazole (or lurasidone/ziprasidone) represents the most metabolically sparing option while maintaining antipsychotic coverage.
10. What weight gain threshold (% of baseline body weight) is typically used to consider switching from olanzapine to a more metabolically neutral agent?
A. 2%
B. 7%
C. 15%
D. 20%
The APA and other guidelines use ≥7% weight gain from baseline as a clinically meaningful threshold that warrants intervention — either adding metformin, implementing structured lifestyle intervention, or switching to a more metabolically neutral antipsychotic such as aripiprazole or lurasidone.
