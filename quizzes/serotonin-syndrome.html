Antidepressants

Serotonin Syndrome & Drug Interactions

10 questions
1. A patient on sertraline is admitted for a urinary tract infection and started on linezolid. Two days later she develops agitation, diaphoresis, tachycardia, and inducible ankle clonus. What is the MOST likely diagnosis?
A. Neuroleptic malignant syndrome
B. Sepsis-related delirium
C. Serotonin syndrome
D. Anticholinergic toxicity
This is classic serotonin syndrome: sertraline (SSRI) + linezolid (weak MAOI) = serotonergic excess. Key features: clonus (the hallmark), agitation, diaphoresis, and tachycardia. Linezolid has monoamine oxidase inhibiting activity — ID teams often miss this interaction. Management: discontinue both agents, supportive care, benzodiazepines, cyproheptadine. NMS features lead-pipe rigidity and elevated CK without clonus.
2. Which physical finding is the HALLMARK of serotonin syndrome (most specific for the diagnosis)?
A. Hyperthermia
B. Clonus
C. Diaphoresis
D. Tachycardia
Clonus — particularly spontaneous or inducible — is the hallmark and most specific feature of serotonin syndrome. It reflects spinal interneuron hyperexcitability from excess serotonin activity. The Hunter Criteria center on clonus as the core diagnostic feature. Hyperthermia, diaphoresis, and tachycardia are shared with many conditions. Always check for inducible ankle clonus (repeated dorsiflexion of the foot) when serotonin syndrome is suspected.
3. A patient on phenelzine for treatment-resistant depression wants to start fluoxetine. They stopped phenelzine 3 days ago. When can they safely start fluoxetine?
A. Now — 3 days is a sufficient washout from phenelzine
B. In 11 more days — total 14 days after stopping phenelzine
C. In 5 weeks — fluoxetine requires a 5-week washout from MAOIs
D. Phenelzine and fluoxetine can be used simultaneously at low doses
After stopping a MAOI (like phenelzine), you must wait at least 14 days before starting any serotonergic antidepressant. Since 3 days have elapsed, an additional 11 days are needed. The 5-week washout applies in the REVERSE direction: fluoxetine's long half-life means you must wait 5 weeks after stopping FLUOXETINE before starting a MAOI. Phenelzine itself requires only 14 days. Combining them at any dose is contraindicated — severe serotonin syndrome risk.
4. Which of the following drug combinations can precipitate serotonin syndrome? (Select all that apply)
A. Sertraline + tramadol
B. Fluoxetine + triptans
C. Phenelzine + meperidine
D. Escitalopram + IV methylene blue
E. Sertraline + acetaminophen
F. Duloxetine + St. John's Wort
Serotonin syndrome can result from any combination that produces serotonergic excess: Sertraline+tramadol (common outpatient combination), SSRI+triptans (5-HT1 agonists — risk especially with MAOIs, lower with SSRIs alone), MAOI+meperidine (historically dangerous — meperidine has serotonergic properties), SSRI+IV methylene blue (potent MAOI activity), SNRI+St. John's Wort (serotonergic supplement). Acetaminophen has no serotonergic activity.
5. First-line pharmacological treatment for serotonin syndrome includes:
A. Bromocriptine and dantrolene
B. Benzodiazepines and cyproheptadine
C. Naloxone and flumazenil
D. Diphenhydramine and benztropine
After discontinuing causative agents, benzodiazepines are first-line for agitation and neuromuscular excitability in serotonin syndrome. Cyproheptadine (Periactin), a 5-HT2A antagonist, can blunt excess serotonin activity (4-8mg PO/NG, max 32mg/day). Physical restraints should be avoided — they worsen hyperthermia and rhabdomyolysis. Bromocriptine and dantrolene are used for NMS. Diphenhydramine/benztropine are for EPS.
6. Maria (58F, sertraline + meperidine post-op) has tremor, agitation, diaphoresis, and temperature 39.2°C. The MOST distinguishing examination finding for serotonin syndrome is:
A. Lead-pipe rigidity
B. Clonus (especially lower extremity) and hyperreflexia
C. Bradykinesia
D. Autonomic instability without hyperreflexia
Serotonin syndrome: clonus + hyperreflexia + agitation + hyperthermia. NMS: lead-pipe rigidity + hyporeflexia + autonomic instability. The Hunter Criteria for serotonin syndrome require clonus (spontaneous, inducible, or ocular) with at least one of: agitation, tremor, diaphoresis, or temperature >38°C.
7. Which medication combination poses the HIGHEST risk for serotonin syndrome?
A. Sertraline + bupropion
B. Fluoxetine + tramadol + linezolid
C. Venlafaxine + mirtazapine
D. Sertraline + trazodone
Fluoxetine (SSRI) + tramadol (weak SNRI + opioid) + linezolid (antibiotic with MAOI properties) = triple serotonergic hit. Linezolid is a reversible MAOI — this combination is genuinely dangerous. Tramadol is a frequently underappreciated serotonergic risk. Mirtazapine with venlafaxine (California Rocket Fuel) is used therapeutically but does increase serotonergic load.
8. Serotonin syndrome typically has what TIME COURSE after the causative agent is added?
A. Days to weeks
B. Within 24 hours, often within 6 hours
C. Only after months of exposure
D. Variable — no typical onset pattern
Serotonin syndrome typically develops within 24 hours of starting or changing a serotonergic medication — often within 6 hours. This rapid onset distinguishes it from NMS, which develops over 24-72 hours. Temporal association with a medication change is critical to the history.
9. The MOST appropriate pharmacological treatment for serotonin syndrome is:
A. Bromocriptine
B. Cyproheptadine
C. Dantrolene
D. Physostigmine
Cyproheptadine (a 5-HT2A antagonist/antihistamine) is used for serotonin syndrome — blocks serotonin receptors. Bromocriptine (dopamine agonist) is used for NMS. Dantrolene (muscle relaxant) used for severe hyperthermia/rigidity in NMS. Physostigmine is used for anticholinergic toxicity.
10. Which of the following medications have MAOI properties and can cause serotonin syndrome when combined with SSRIs? Select ALL that apply.
A. Linezolid (antibiotic)
B. Methylene blue (IV)
C. Tramadol
D. St. John's Wort
E. Fentanyl
Linezolid = reversible MAOI (antibiotic). Methylene blue IV = potent MAO inhibitor. St. John's Wort = serotonergic + CYP interactions. Tramadol = serotonergic (weak SNRI + opioid) but not an MAOI. Fentanyl has minimal serotonergic activity unlike meperidine or tramadol.
