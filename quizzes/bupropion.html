Bupropion (Wellbutrin): The Dopaminergic Antidepressant

10 questions
1. Bupropion is CONTRAINDICATED in patients with bulimia nervosa because:
A. It causes serotonin syndrome with purging behaviors
B. Electrolyte imbalances from purging increase seizure risk — bupropion significantly lowers the seizure threshold
C. It causes weight gain in eating disorder patients
D. It is contraindicated in all patients with psychiatric comorbidities
Bupropion carries a dose-dependent seizure risk. In patients with bulimia or anorexia, purging behaviors cause electrolyte imbalances (hypokalemia, hyponatremia) and hypoglycemia that further lower the seizure threshold. This combination is associated with a 3–4× increased seizure risk — making bupropion contraindicated in active eating disorders.
2. Bupropion is weight-neutral or weight-reducing because of which mechanism?
A. It causes nausea, reducing caloric intake
B. Dopaminergic activity in the reward circuit reduces food-related reward seeking; noradrenergic activity suppresses appetite
C. It increases metabolic rate via thyroid stimulation
D. It directly inhibits fat absorption
Bupropion's dopaminergic and noradrenergic activity (without serotonergic effects) reduces appetite via hypothalamic circuits and food-reward pathways. Unlike serotonergic antidepressants (which can increase 5-HT2C-mediated appetite suppression but cause long-term weight gain via other mechanisms), bupropion is weight-neutral to weight-reducing.
3. A patient on an SSRI is experiencing anorgasmia and decreased libido. The BEST augmentation strategy is:
A. Add aripiprazole
B. Switch to mirtazapine
C. Add bupropion SR 150–300 mg — dopaminergic activity counteracts SSRI sexual dysfunction
D. Increase the SSRI dose
Adding bupropion to an SSRI is a well-evidenced strategy for SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction. Bupropion's dopaminergic activity augments sexual function without reducing antidepressant efficacy. Evidence from RCTs supports this combination. Typical dose: bupropion SR 150–300 mg/day added to the existing SSRI.
4. Bupropion is marketed as Zyban for smoking cessation. At what dose and timing relative to quit date is it typically started?
A. 150 mg SR BID, started 1–2 weeks before quit date
B. 300 mg XL QAM, started day of quit
C. 75 mg TID, started day of quit
D. 450 mg XL once daily, 4 weeks before quit date
For smoking cessation, bupropion SR 150 mg is started 1–2 weeks BEFORE the designated quit date to achieve therapeutic levels. After 1 week, the dose is increased to 150 mg BID. The pre-quit start allows the medication to begin reducing nicotine craving and withdrawal severity before the patient stops smoking.
5. What is the maximum recommended daily dose of bupropion?
A. 300 mg
B. 375 mg
C. 450 mg
D. 600 mg
The maximum recommended dose of bupropion is 450 mg/day (as Wellbutrin XL 450 mg [Forfivo XL] or Wellbutrin SR 150 mg TID). The seizure risk increases non-linearly above 450 mg/day. No single dose of IR bupropion should exceed 150 mg to reduce peak-concentration-associated seizure risk.
6. Bupropion is a CYP2D6 inhibitor. Which drug interaction is MOST clinically relevant?
A. Bupropion reduces SSRI levels, requiring dose increases
B. Bupropion raises TCAs and beta-blocker levels — dose reductions may be needed when combining
C. Bupropion blocks lithium clearance
D. Bupropion reduces warfarin levels
As a moderate-to-strong CYP2D6 inhibitor, bupropion significantly raises plasma levels of drugs metabolized by CYP2D6, including tricyclic antidepressants, certain antipsychotics (haloperidol, risperidone, aripiprazole), metoprolol, and codeine (reduced conversion to morphine, reducing efficacy). Dose reductions of co-administered CYP2D6 substrates may be required.
7. Bupropion is contraindicated with concurrent MAOI use. What is the required washout period?
A. 24 hours
B. 3 days
C. 14 days
D. 5 weeks
Like all antidepressants with monoaminergic activity, bupropion requires a 14-day washout period before or after MAOI use. Combining bupropion with an MAOI can cause hypertensive crisis and serotonin syndrome due to combined dopamine/NE elevation and reduced catecholamine breakdown.
8. Bupropion is unique among antidepressants for what property related to sexual function?
A. It causes sexual dysfunction more commonly than SSRIs
B. It has no serotonergic activity — it does NOT cause serotonin-mediated sexual dysfunction
C. It enhances sexual function only in men, not women
D. It requires co-prescription of PDE5 inhibitors for efficacy
Bupropion's mechanism (NDRI — norepinephrine and dopamine reuptake inhibition) completely lacks serotonergic activity. Since SSRI/SNRI sexual dysfunction is primarily serotonin-mediated, bupropion does not cause this side effect and is the preferred antidepressant when sexual function is a priority.
9. Bupropion has evidence as a second-line treatment for which comorbid condition in adults?
A. PTSD
B. Bipolar I disorder
C. Adult ADHD
D. Panic disorder
Bupropion has evidence as a second-line pharmacological treatment for ADHD in adults (after stimulants and atomoxetine). Its dopaminergic and noradrenergic mechanisms address the core dopamine/NE dysregulation in ADHD. It is particularly useful when ADHD co-occurs with depression or substance use disorders where stimulants may be problematic.
10. Bupropion is FDA-approved for seasonal affective disorder (SAD) in which specific formulation and timing?
A. IR 100 mg TID, started in summer
B. XL 150–300 mg, started in autumn before onset of seasonal depression
C. SR 150 mg BID, started only after SAD symptoms appear
D. Bupropion is not FDA-approved for SAD
Bupropion XL is FDA-approved for the prevention of major depressive episodes associated with seasonal affective disorder (SAD). Treatment is started in autumn before the typical onset of seasonal depressive symptoms and continued until spring. This preventive strategy is unique among antidepressant indications.
