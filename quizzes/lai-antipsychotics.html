Long-Acting Injectable (LAI) Antipsychotics

10 questions
1. Which long-acting injectable antipsychotic requires a 3-hour post-injection observation period due to risk of post-injection delirium/sedation syndrome?
A. Paliperidone palmitate (Invega Sustenna)
B. Aripiprazole lauroxil (Aristada)
C. Olanzapine pamoate (Zyprexa Relprevv)
D. Risperidone microspheres (Risperdal Consta)
Olanzapine pamoate carries a ~0.07% risk of post-injection delirium/sedation syndrome (PDSS), characterized by excessive sedation, delirium, and/or dizziness occurring within hours of injection. All patients must be observed for 3 hours following each injection at a registered healthcare facility. No other commonly used LAI requires this observation period. This requirement limits the practical use of olanzapine LAI in many settings.
2. Which LAI antipsychotic does NOT require an oral antipsychotic overlap when initiating treatment?
A. Risperidone microspheres (Risperdal Consta)
B. Haloperidol decanoate
C. Paliperidone palmitate (Invega Sustenna)
D. Fluphenazine decanoate
Paliperidone palmitate (Invega Sustenna) uses a loading dose strategy (deltoid injections on day 1 and day 8) that achieves therapeutic levels quickly without requiring an oral antipsychotic overlap. Risperidone microspheres (Consta) require 3 weeks of oral overlap because drug release from microspheres is delayed. Most other LAIs also require some overlap period.
3. The primary clinical advantage of long-acting injectable antipsychotics over oral formulations is:
A. Significantly greater antipsychotic efficacy per molecule
B. Elimination of covert nonadherence, reducing relapse rates
C. Absence of metabolic side effects
D. Availability for all antipsychotic medications
The primary advantage of LAIs is the elimination of covert nonadherence — when the injection is missed, you know exactly when nonadherence occurred. Nonadherence is the #1 cause of psychiatric relapse in schizophrenia. Meta-analyses show LAIs reduce relapse rates by approximately 25-30% compared to oral equivalents. LAIs have the same metabolic profiles as their oral counterparts, are not available for all antipsychotics, and don't have superior molecular efficacy.
4. How frequently is paliperidone palmitate 3-month (Invega Trinza) administered?
A. Every 2 weeks
B. Every month
C. Every 3 months
D. Every 6 months
Invega Trinza (paliperidone palmitate 3-month formulation) is administered every 3 months after the patient has been stable on monthly Invega Sustenna for at least 4 months. This extended interval represents the longest-acting injectable antipsychotic currently available in the US, further reducing injection burden and potentially improving quality of life.
5. When is it MOST appropriate to offer a long-acting injectable antipsychotic?
A. Only after multiple relapses due to nonadherence
B. Only for involuntary patients in institutional settings
C. Early in treatment, including after first-episode psychosis, as a preference-based option
D. Only when oral medications have been completely exhausted
Current evidence supports offering LAIs early in treatment — including after first-episode psychosis — rather than as a last resort. Studies in first-episode psychosis show improved outcomes with LAIs. LAIs should be presented as a patient-centered option for anyone who might benefit from reducing the daily pill burden or who has a history of nonadherence, not reserved for chronically ill patients in institutional settings.
6. Jose (35M) is stabilized on paliperidone 6mg/day and interested in switching to the LAI. What is the CORRECT initiation protocol for paliperidone palmitate 1-month (PP1M)?
A. Give 234mg deltoid on Day 1, 156mg deltoid on Day 8, then monthly 117mg maintenance
B. Stop oral paliperidone immediately and give 150mg monthly starting today
C. Overlap oral paliperidone for 3 months while starting the monthly injection
D. Give a loading dose then switch to quarterly injections immediately
PP1M loading protocol: 234mg deltoid (Day 1) + 156mg deltoid (Day 8) — this achieves therapeutic levels quickly without oral overlap. Subsequent monthly doses are individualized (75-234mg). Improper initiation risks subtherapeutic levels and relapse.
7. Which LAI requires a 3-hour post-injection observation period at a registered healthcare facility after EVERY injection?
A. Haloperidol decanoate
B. Paliperidone palmitate
C. Olanzapine pamoate (Zyprexa Relprevv)
D. Risperidone microspheres
Olanzapine pamoate has a unique risk of post-injection delirium/sedation syndrome (PDSS) — occurring in ~0.07% of injections. Symptoms include confusion, dizziness, and altered consciousness, likely from unintentional intravascular injection. The 3-hour observation requirement is mandatory per its REMS program.
8. A patient on haloperidol decanoate 100mg IM monthly has a breakthrough psychotic exacerbation 3 weeks post-injection. The MOST appropriate first step is:
A. Double the next monthly dose.
B. Add oral haloperidol as a bridge, review adherence, and reassess dose.
C. Switch to a different antipsychotic immediately.
D. Hospitalize for stabilization on clozapine.
Breakthrough psychosis near the end of an injection cycle ('end-of-cycle failure') suggests the dose or interval is inadequate. Bridge with oral antipsychotic while reviewing the overall regimen. Immediate dose-doubling or switching without understanding the cause is premature.
9. Which are TRUE advantages of long-acting injectable antipsychotics compared to oral formulations? Select ALL that apply.
A. Eliminates variability from oral absorption
B. Provides objective evidence of non-adherence
C. Eliminates all side effects
D. Reduces hospitalization rates in patients with poor adherence
E. Ensures steady-state plasma levels
LAIs provide predictable pharmacokinetics, objective adherence monitoring (missed appointment = non-adherence), reduced hospitalization rates, and stable plasma levels. They do NOT eliminate side effects — side effects from the base compound still occur.
10. What is the minimum duration of oral antipsychotic overlap typically required when initiating risperidone microspheres (Risperdal Consta)?
A. No overlap needed
B. 3 weeks
C. 3-4 weeks oral overlap (due to delayed release)
D. 8 weeks
Risperidone microspheres (Consta) have a unique pharmacokinetics — the microsphere matrix delays peak plasma concentration by ~3 weeks. A 3-4 week oral overlap is required to maintain therapeutic levels during initiation. This is unlike paliperidone palmitate, which uses a loading protocol for immediate coverage.
