Anxiolytics & Sleep

Sleep Pharmacology: Z-drugs, Trazodone & More

10 questions
1. An 80-year-old with insomnia is asking for a sleep medication. Which agent is MOST appropriate per Beers Criteria?
A. Zolpidem 10mg QHS
B. Lorazepam 0.5mg QHS
C. Ramelteon 8mg QHS
D. Diphenhydramine 50mg QHS
Ramelteon (melatonin receptor agonist) is the safest option in elderly patients — it is not on the Beers Criteria and has no dependence potential, no CNS depression, and no anticholinergic effects. Zolpidem and lorazepam are explicitly on the Beers Criteria for elderly due to fall and fracture risk. Diphenhydramine is also on Beers Criteria (anticholinergic effects — cognitive impairment, urinary retention).
2. Which class of sleep medications works by blocking orexin (hypocretin) receptors to promote sleep onset and maintenance?
A. Z-drugs (zolpidem, eszopiclone)
B. Melatonin receptor agonists
C. Orexin receptor antagonists (suvorexant, lemborexant)
D. Benzodiazepines
Suvorexant (Belsomra) and lemborexant (Dayvigo) are orexin receptor antagonists — they block the wake-promoting orexin/hypocretin system rather than sedating the entire CNS. This mechanism is distinct from GABA-A modulating agents (benzodiazepines, Z-drugs) and melatonin agonists. They may cause vivid dreams and have lower dependence potential than Z-drugs, making them a useful alternative.
3. Trazodone is commonly used off-label for insomnia. Which property makes it preferable to benzodiazepines for chronic insomnia?
A. Faster onset of sleep than benzodiazepines
B. No physical dependence or withdrawal syndrome
C. Stronger evidence for sleep maintenance insomnia
D. FDA approval specifically for insomnia at low doses
Trazodone's key advantage for chronic insomnia is the absence of physical dependence and withdrawal syndrome — unlike benzodiazepines and Z-drugs which can cause tolerance, dependence, and rebound insomnia on discontinuation. Trazodone's sedating properties come from 5-HT2A and H1 receptor antagonism. Key cautions: orthostatic hypotension and rare priapism (painful sustained erection — medical emergency).
4. What is the FIRST-LINE treatment for chronic insomnia disorder?
A. Zolpidem (Ambien)
B. Low-dose quetiapine
C. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)
D. Melatonin supplements
CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) is universally recommended as first-line treatment for chronic insomnia by major sleep societies (AASM, American College of Physicians). CBT-I includes stimulus control, sleep restriction therapy, sleep hygiene, and cognitive restructuring. Multiple meta-analyses show superior long-term outcomes compared to pharmacotherapy. Medications are appropriate as adjuncts or when CBT-I is unavailable/ineffective.
5. Which sleep medications are listed on the AGS Beers Criteria and should be AVOIDED in older adults? (Select all that apply)
A. Zolpidem (Ambien)
B. Eszopiclone (Lunesta)
C. Ramelteon (Rozerem)
D. Temazepam (Restoril)
E. Diphenhydramine (Benadryl)
F. Low-dose doxepin (Silenor 3-6mg)
Beers Criteria for older adults: all benzodiazepines (including temazepam) and all Z-drugs (zolpidem, eszopiclone, zaleplon) are listed due to fall/fracture risk and cognitive impairment. Diphenhydramine is listed due to anticholinergic effects (cognitive impairment, delirium, urinary retention). Ramelteon and low-dose doxepin (3-6mg) are NOT on Beers Criteria and are acceptable options in elderly.
6. Z-drugs (zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone) are classified as non-benzodiazepine GABA-A modulators. What is their MAIN clinical advantage over traditional benzodiazepines for insomnia?
A. They are not associated with any dependence
B. More selective for α1 GABA-A subunits (associated with sleep) vs. α2 (anxiolytic/euphoric) — potentially less abuse potential
C. They have longer half-lives providing sustained sleep
D. No rebound insomnia on discontinuation
Z-drugs selectively target α1-containing GABA-A receptors (which mediate sedation/sleep) over α2/3 subunits (which mediate anxiolysis and reinforcement/euphoria). This relative selectivity may reduce abuse potential compared to benzodiazepines. However, they are still Schedule IV controlled substances and can cause dependence, especially with long-term use.
7. Trazodone for insomnia works primarily through which mechanism?
A. GABA-A potentiation
B. H1 antagonism — sedating at low doses; SERT inhibition at higher antidepressant doses
C. Melatonin receptor agonism
D. Orexin receptor antagonism
Trazodone's sedating properties at low doses (25–100 mg) come from H1 histamine receptor blockade and α1 adrenergic blockade. Its serotonin reuptake inhibition becomes clinically relevant at antidepressant doses (150–400 mg). Low-dose trazodone (25–100 mg QHS) is widely used off-label for insomnia with minimal next-day sedation and no dependence risk.
8. Suvorexant (Belsomra) and lemborexant (Dayvigo) treat insomnia by which novel mechanism?
A. GABA-A potentiation like benzodiazepines
B. Blocking orexin (hypocretin) receptors OX1R and OX2R — reducing wakefulness-promoting signals
C. Increasing melatonin secretion
D. Adenosine receptor agonism
Orexin antagonists block OX1R and OX2R, reducing the wakefulness-promoting effects of orexin (hypocretin). This 'turns off' the arousal signal rather than nonspecifically sedating the brain. Advantages: minimal rebound insomnia, no significant respiratory depression, no significant abuse potential, Beers-criteria-safer in elderly than benzodiazepines or Z-drugs.
9. Which insomnia medication is most appropriate for an 80-year-old patient to avoid falls and cognitive impairment?
A. Zolpidem 10 mg QHS
B. Temazepam 30 mg QHS
C. Low-dose doxepin (Silenor 3–6 mg) or suvorexant
D. Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) 50 mg QHS
In elderly patients, benzodiazepines and Z-drugs are on the Beers Criteria as potentially inappropriate due to falls, cognitive impairment, and next-day sedation risk. Low-dose doxepin (Silenor 3–6 mg) — FDA-approved for sleep maintenance — has a minimal side effect profile at these doses. Suvorexant (orexin antagonist) also has a favorable profile in elderly. Diphenhydramine causes anticholinergic toxicity in elderly.
10. CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) is considered the FIRST-LINE treatment for chronic insomnia over pharmacotherapy because:
A. It works faster than any medication
B. Long-term outcomes are superior to sleep medications, with no dependence, withdrawal, or tolerance
C. It is cheaper than all medications
D. Medications are not effective for insomnia
CBT-I has superior long-term outcomes compared to all pharmacological treatments for chronic insomnia. It addresses the underlying perpetuating factors (sleep hygiene beliefs, conditioned arousal, sleep restriction paradox) rather than just symptom suppression. Unlike sleep medications, CBT-I has no risk of tolerance, dependence, withdrawal, or next-day cognitive impairment.
